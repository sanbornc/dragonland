## What we know
- People (or beings) the Ringmaster traffics as "special cargo": "Miracle-bearers draw eyes. Old gods, new lies, same price if delivered breathing." ([Ringmaster's Ledger — Special Cargo / Things Not To Say](../documents/ringmasters-ledger-special-cargo.md))
- Tell-tale signs to hide them: "no blue light, no singing under the floorboards." ([Ringmaster's Ledger — Special Cargo / Things Not To Say](../documents/ringmasters-ledger-special-cargo.md))
- Linked to a tension between "old gods" and "new lies." ([Ringmaster's Ledger — Special Cargo / Things Not To Say](../documents/ringmasters-ledger-special-cargo.md))
- **Likely meaning:** in a world where [true divine power is believed long gone](divine-power.md),
  a miracle-bearer is someone whose prayers genuinely *answer* — capable of real
  holy miracles (true healing, life returning by divine grace). That rarity is
  what makes them valuable, dangerous "cargo." (GM background — [Boilerdang](../characters/boilerdang-criddlycrotch.md))
- The party's own [Boilerdang Criddlycrotch](../characters/boilerdang-criddlycrotch.md)
  fits this description: his cleric magic genuinely works, which is almost unheard of.

## Open questions
