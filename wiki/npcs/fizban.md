# Fizban

A **wizard** the party met in the **[Inn of the Last Home](../locations/inn-of-the-last-home.md)**
in [Solace](../locations/solace.md) (Session 04). First seen as an unnamed lute-playing
patron; he later gave his name as **Fizban**. Drunk, mischievous, powerful, and clearly
a **knowing player** in the Staff story — not a random patron.

## What we know

- A **wizard** among the inn's patrons; goes by **Fizban.** Presents as a **jovial,
  eccentric drunk** with a mischievous streak. (Session 04)
- He **produced a lute and invited [Goldmoon](goldmoon.md) to sing** — and she sang
  **[The Song of Riverwind](../documents/song-of-riverwind.md)**, the ballad of her own
  story and the blue [Staff](../items/the-staff.md). (Session 04)
- When [Hederick](high-theocrat-hederick.md) fell into the fire, **he handed
  [Boilerdang](../characters/boilerdang-criddlycrotch.md) the [Staff](../items/the-staff.md)** —
  **Goldmoon's Staff** — with which Boilerdang **healed** the High Theocrat's burns. So
  he **knew what the Staff was, knew it could heal, and had it to hand.** (Session 04)
- **He did not get the Staff from Goldmoon willingly** — she did not consent to him
  taking/using it, so he is **not simply her agent.** (Session 04)
- **He is a capable spellcaster.** When [Boilerdang](../characters/boilerdang-criddlycrotch.md)
  cast **Hold Person** on Hederick and **[Malix](../characters/malix.md)** **Webbed the
  door** (to keep the guards out), Fizban **dispelled the party's magic** — with a
  **drunken wink** at Boilerdang. (Session 04)
- **He has a sense of humor about himself.** When **[Renob](../characters/renob.md)** drew
  a **crude ash phallus on Fizban's head**, Fizban **loved it** and asked for **one on his
  back as well.** (Session 04)

## Open questions

- **Who is Fizban, really?** A drunk who can dispel a cleric's and a sorcerer's spells,
  knows Goldmoon and the Staff intimately, and steers events from a bar stool is hiding
  something. Mentor, trickster, or something stranger?
- How does he **know Goldmoon and the Staff** so well, and **how did he come to hold it**
  (against her will)? What is his claim or interest in it?
- Was the whole sequence — the song, the fire, the public healing — **engineered** by
  him? To what end? Is he protecting Goldmoon, the Staff, or pursuing his own design?
- Whose **side** is he on regarding the Seekers, Hederick, and the hunt for the Staff?
